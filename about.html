The position

A foot in two worlds.

One grounded in organic beauty, proportion, craft and human experience. The other at the edge of technology, automation, AI and digital practice.

Most AI consulting comes from people who only live in the second world. They can tell you what the tools do; they cannot tell you whether the output is any good, because they have never had to defend a drawing set, hit a deadline, or answer to a client about why a rendering does not match the site. Almost everyone selling AI to this profession has never practised in it.

That is the whole premise here. The work gets tested on real projects, in a real studio, by people who have to live with the results.

Why these backgrounds

Who ends up leading AI in a design firm.

New roles are emerging across the workforce centred on AI research and implementation. In practice, the people who end up carrying it tend to come from three places:

  • Technology and coding backgrounds
  • 3D modelling and spatial-thinking backgrounds
  • Drone and remote-sensing backgrounds

It is rarely who the org chart would predict. Finding those people inside your firm — and giving them the deeper training — is what the AiT idea is for.
