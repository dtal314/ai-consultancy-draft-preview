One spine, three stages.

Every engagement below sits somewhere on it. Most firms want to start at the third stage. Almost none of them should — which is the single most useful thing we can tell you before you spend anything.

Stage one

Understand

What these tools are, in plain language. Which bots do what, which ones may merit a subscription and why, and what a token actually is — because it is the unit your costs are denominated in.

Literacy across the firm. Then literacy where it counts.

Stage two

Adopt

Governed use. Policy, security, privacy, liability. Bounded pilots on real work, measured honestly. Training for the people who will carry it, including designated AiT champions who organize and track use.

One workflow, done repeatably, beats ten demos.

Stage three

Build

Selective insourcing. Custom apps aimed at defined bottlenecks, agents and automation, visualization pipelines, and tools built to your standards — not a vendor's idea of your standards.

Only for a bottleneck you have already measured.

Common questions

About these services.

What does AI 101 cover?

AI 101 covers the LLM ecosystem of bots, which bots are worth subscriptions and why, general writing with AI including emails, proposals and memos, deep searches on specific topics, general AI literacy for the firm, specific AI literacy for particular people within the firm, and how to organize and keep track of AI use within your firm. It is the base layer everything else sits on.

What is AEO and how is it different from SEO?

AEO is answer engine optimization: structuring a website so AI answer engines can read, understand and cite it. SEO gets you found; AEO gets you quoted. It covers the basic needs that make a website searchable by AI, becoming the proven expert for AI to find, and specific approaches for optimization. The difference matters because people increasingly ask an assistant instead of scanning ten blue links — and the assistant only mentions who it can read.
